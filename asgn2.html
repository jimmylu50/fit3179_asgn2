World Development: The Positive and Negative Consequences

Positive Factors

As the world continues to progress, there is a balance of opposite results. GDP Per Capita is one of the most important positive indicators which reflects a nation's development in the economic sense.

 

The map shows that developed countries have the best economic development, which indicates that their citizens are doing better than most people in the world. In contrast, large amount of underdeveloped countries have low GDP Per Capita, showing that they have poorer life quality in comparison to other nations.

On the other hand, General Necessity is another indicator of looking at how different nations have developed over the years.

 

Basic Sanitation is definitely one of the impacting factors which reflect living quality of people. As shown in the line graph on the right, it indicates that there is a steady increase in Basic Sanitation level across different regions.

 

It is also clear to see that there is a significant increase in Basic Sanitation Level for underdeveloped regions compared to first-world continents. This demonstrates that life quality of people are slowly improving across the globe.

Negative Factors

As for negative consequences of world development, more CO2 Emission had to be made for the improvement of human life quality.

 

In the interactive scatterplot, we can see that the population and CO2 Emission at each nation seem to be positively correlated.

 

When we scroll through the past 20 years, it is clear that with more population, more CO2 is emitted.

 

However, we can see that as we advance through the years, the life expectancy increases in almost all nations due to better life quality.

CO2 Emission is the one of the components that are responsible for global warming and it is produced from burning of fossil fuels, which is frequently done for various uses in human lives.

 

It is clear that East Asia has seen the greatest increase in CO2 Emission across all continents in the world. This is most likely due to the rise of China becoming that world factory and producing more products than ever.

 

Apart from China, most of the continents have a steady increase of CO2 Emission over the past decades, which further imposes the danger of global warming on future generations.

The legend of the stacked area chart is interactive.

Relationships between the factors

In the interactive scatterplot, it presents correlations between the factors of "GDP Per Capita" in USD, "CO2 Emission Per Capita" in Tonnes and "Life Expectancy".

 

As shown, there is a strong positive correlation between "Life Expectancy" and "GDP Per Capita". It indicates that nations with high GDP per capita have better medical services and life quality , aiding in prolonging their citizens' life expectancy. These are often the developed, first-world countries.

 

On the other hand, there is a somewhat positive correlation between GDP Per Capita and CO2 Emission Per Capita. However, some countries, such as Denmark, have high GDP Per Capita, but maintain a low CO2 Emission Per Capita.

 

Ultimately, it can be concluded that with general world development, there are positive and negative impacts to the world and life quality of humans. We should aim to achieve a good balance between the positives and negatives, and make the future brighter all generations at stake.
